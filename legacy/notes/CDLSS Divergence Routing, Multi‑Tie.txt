CDLSS: Divergence Routing, Multi‑Tier Memory, and Post‑Storm Synthesis
1. Cache as the Live Storm Zone
All trajectories begin in cache.
This is where speculative reasoning happens:
• 	token‑by‑token expansion
• 	divergence
• 	cluster formation
• 	DCX scoring
• 	early runner emergence
Cache is fast, local, and ephemeral — perfect for active computation.
2. Three Fates for Trajectories
During the storm, each trajectory ends up in one of three categories:
A. Cluster Keepers (stay in cache)
These remain close to the dominant attractor.
They form the basis of the main ensemble collapse.
B. Exceptional Outliers (routed to RAM)
These are not “correct” or “coherent” by default.
They are simply structurally different enough to be worth preserving.
They are evacuated from cache and moved to RAM, where they continue running to completion without freezing or competing for cache residency.
C. True Prunes (destroyed)
These are neither useful to the cluster nor interesting as outliers.
They are annihilated to keep cache from bloating.
3. RAM as the Cold Archive
RAM stores:
• 	completed cluster trajectories
• 	completed outlier trajectories
• 	partial divergent fragments that were evacuated early
Everything that survives the storm ends up here.
Nothing interesting is lost.
RAM becomes a “shadow dataset” of:
• 	minority clusters
• 	alternate interpretations
• 	early‑stage deviations
• 	hallucination fragments with potential value
4. No Freeze, No Deadlock
Because divergent paths are routed to RAM instead of being forced to collapse:
• 	the storm never stalls
• 	cache never overflows
• 	all trajectories finish naturally
• 	the system remains stable under heavy divergence
5. Second‑Pass Evaluation
Once all trajectories complete, the expensive part is over.
Now you can afford to read instead of predict.
A second pass — using the same model or another — performs:
• 	cross‑trajectory fact confirmation
• 	extraction of unique but verifiable details
• 	elimination of unverifiable or contradictory junk
This produces a 4th ensemble output that is:
• 	more grounded
• 	more diverse
• 	more cross‑validated
• 	strictly higher quality than any single trajectory
6. Efficiency Advantage
Even if the total wall‑clock time matches a single pass on a full‑sized model, CDLSS wins because:
• 	you harvest many trajectories instead of one
• 	you preserve non‑dominant structure
• 	you perform verification across a full speculative field
• 	you collapse only after seeing the entire space of “what the model almost said”
This guarantees that CDLSS outputs are higher quality than the original model’s output every time.
